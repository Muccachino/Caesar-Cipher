Caesar Cipher

The Caesar Cipher encrypts a text by shifting the letters by a specified number in the alphabet.
For example the word "Hello" would be "Mjqqt" if you shift it by the value 5.
To use this cipher you write your text in the left area and type a number between 1 and 25 in the small middle area.
To decipher a text you also put your text in the left area, but type a negative number as shift value (e.g. -5).
